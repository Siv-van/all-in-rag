## In many parts of the world the streets are full of cats, where do they all come from?

Helping individual cats is very rewarding but if we want to help the whole population, we need to ask ‘bigger picture’ questions such as this.

## Why are there so many cats?

If there are no population management solutions for street cats, or those in place are ineffective, cats are not neutered and are therefore breeding successfully and numbers are increasing

If there is a freely available food supply (people are feeding unneutered cats) and/or ineffective food waste disposal, this will ensure that cats have enough nutrition to reproduce

If the pet cat population is not neutered in sufficient numbers and is breeding, unwanted pets and kittens are entering the community of street cats when they are abandoned

If the street dog population is well managed, and predation on cats is reduced, then numbers of street cats may increase

The importance of neutering

Solutions should consider all these causes and try to stop the source rather than constantly tackle the symptoms of the underlying problems. Neutering of all cats stops more cats being added to the problem. Collaboration on the importance of neutering needs to be emphasised by

The local community

Government – local/national

Animal organisations (eg a community-based group of volunteers, an individual organisation/charity/NGO, or a coalition of organisations)

Wildlife organisations

Vets

Local businesses

The tourist sector

Until neutering is more widely undertaken the root cause of the problems will not be removed.
