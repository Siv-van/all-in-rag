# TNVR - Equipment checklist

## Source
https://icatcare.org/articles/tnvr-equipment-checklist

---

All TNVR programs rely on good preparation to ensure the safety and well-being of both cats and caretakers. Having the right equipment on hand can make the process smoother, more efficient, and less stressful for everyone involved. This checklist covers the essential tools and materials you’ll need for a successful and humane TNVR operation.

Vehicle

The vehicle may see a lot of wear and tear during the TNVR process, so the owner must be aware of this. Drive slowly and gently to accommodate the cats. Make sure there is parking near the site, and consider how far you may have to carry your traps and cats.

Materials to protect, cover and absorb

You will need a large supply of

Plastic or cotton sheets

Newspaper

Towels

Puppy pads

These can be used to cover traps and protect the vehicle’s surfaces from urine and other bodily fluids that are an inevitable part of the trapping process. You can also use these between layers of traps if you are stacking them. You may also like to consider carrying pegs or tape to fix the newspaper to the traps (especially important on a windy day!)

Feeding utensils and food

#### You will need a supply of the following

- Disposable dishes and clip-on water containers

- Tasty food with a strong odour to tempt cats, eg, tinned tuna, chicken, tinned sardines or mackerel, sausage, ham, steamed fish and catnip (a dried herb that is very attractive to many cats)

- Tab-opening cans or sachets are the easiest to use; otherwise, make sure you have a can opener

- Containers to store open but unused food

- Forks or spoons

- Equipment to maintain and secure traps and carriers

#### To secure traps and carriers, you will need

Cable ties, bungee cords

Knife or scissors

String for manual traps

Tools to carry out minor repairs to traps

Bags for any waste that needs to be taken away

Equipment to support people’s safety

#### To support people’s safety, ensure you have

Wet wipes (antibacterial) or gel, and paper towels

Gloves that are thick enough to protect the handler when carrying a trap

First aid kit

Photo ID and any relevant documentation to show who you are and that you have permission to be there

Refreshments for the trappers

For a significant TNVR programme, t-shirts or badges can help the public identify those associated with the programme

Equipment to see the problem

#### Helpful visual equipment includes

- Torches and spare batteries

- Binoculars if the cats are some distance away. Night vision goggles can also be useful, but only if there is some degree of light source

- Recording equipment

#### Necessary recording equipment includes

- Paperwork to record details of each cat, along with pens/pencils.

- A mobile phone or tablet can be used to take photos and record information
