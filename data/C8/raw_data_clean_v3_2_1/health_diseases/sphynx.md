# Sphynx

## Source
https://icatcare.org/articles/sphynx

---

The main feature of a Sphynx is its lack of a fur coat. The cats are not entirely hairless, however, but covered with fine, downy hair that is said to be like peach skin. The Sphynx has no whiskers or eyelashes. The skin is wrinkled on parts of the head, body and legs but should be taut everywhere else. Despite their lack of hair, Sphynx cats can still cause allergic reactions, as allergies to cats are usually caused by an allergen in feline saliva.

Conditions seen in this breed

Devon RexMyopathy and paroxysmal dyskinesia (movement disorder) have also been found in Sphynx cats. Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy (HCM) is strongly suspected to be inherited in this breed, and they are also likely to suffer other types ofheart disorder (e.g. mitral valve dysplasia), skin diseases (including increased Malassezia (yeast) carriage, ear infections, figurate erythema and maculopapular cutaneous mastocytosis,), ocular sequestrum (a discoloured piece on the otherwise transparent layer that covers the eye), uterine infections and urate urinary stones. In aJFMSstudy, Sphynx had the shortest life expectancy compared to all other breeds.

## Causes

The causes of the disorders described above are most likely genetic, however, we have limited information about inherited disorders in many cat breeds.

In general, pedigree breeds use a much smaller gene pool for breeding than domestic cats and therefore have a higher risk of developing inherited disorders. In addition, several ‘newer’ pedigree breeds are derived from matings between one or more ‘older’ breeds, and in these situations, inherited problems that were seen in older breeds are also likely within the newer breeds.

Our Position on this breed

International Cat Care’s position is that for a cat, a coat is essential. We should not be breeding hairless cats.

To find out what genetic diseases can be tested in each cat breed, and which laboratories are recommended by WSAVA´s Inherited Disease Committee, follow this link to thePennVet University of Pennsylvania website.
