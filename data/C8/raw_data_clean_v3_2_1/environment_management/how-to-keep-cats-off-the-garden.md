# How to keep cats off the garden

## Source
https://icatcare.org/articles/how-to-keep-cats-off-the-garden

---

It can be frustrating when neighbourhood cats are digging up freshly planted areas of the garden; this guidance explores how cats can be humanely kept off your garden.

## Why is my neighbour’s cat toileting in my garden?

Finding cat faeces in your garden can be irritating and somewhat off-putting when you want fresh vegetables, but local cats are using your garden simply because you’ve made that patch of soil very attractive to them. Cats naturally dig a hole in the soil to deposit their urine or faeces and then cover it up. So, if their own garden is frozen, rock hard, impacted or paved, they will go elsewhere to find a place that allows them to fulfil their natural needs.

## How do I keep cats off my garden?

#### People try lots of things to keep cats off gardens and although there is no guaranteed way to achieve success, some of these methods may help

Put pruned branches from spiky plants around and over the patch, especially as seedlings come through, or plant shrubs close together

Place pebbles and stones in between plants, as cats naturally avoid these

PlantColeous caninaaround the patch (said to be offensive to cats and available from garden centres), lavender, or place orange and lemon peels directly on the soil (cats don’t like the smell of lavender or citrus)

Sprinkle commercially available cat deterrents (though these can get washed away when it rains)

Put chicken wire over the soil once you’ve planted seeds. Seedlings can grow through it very successfully and it prevents the soil from being dug up. Alternatively, try a mesh barrier over the seedlings

Provide a cat friendly garden space, with an area of light, raked soil or sand to use as a toilet away from the main vegetable plot

Cat friendly toilet provided for a cat in the garden with soft sand

Also, always remember to cover children’s sandpits or boxes when not used. These are very tempting for cats as they have soft sand, which is easy to dig in and cover over.
