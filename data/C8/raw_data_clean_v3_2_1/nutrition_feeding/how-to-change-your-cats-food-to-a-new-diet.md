# How to change your cat’s food to a new diet

## Source
https://icatcare.org/articles/how-to-change-your-cats-food-to-a-new-diet

---

There are many reasons why you might want or need to change your cat's diet, and there are a few general rules you can follow for success.

Why you might need to make a change

They need to change to a different life stage diet as they mature, as cats have different nutritional needs at each life stage

They may be diagnosed with a health condition that requires a dietary change as part of their treatment plan

They may require a short-term diet change when they are unwell that your vet may recommend to help them recover

General tips for success

#### Regardless of the reason, unless your vet has given you some very specific advice on how to introduce the new diet, there are a few general rules you can follow

It is better to change from an old diet to a new diet gradually to help prevent gastrointestinal upset, such as vomiting and/or diarrhoea

Allow the cat to set the pace. Some cats may accept a new diet over 5-7 days, whilst others may take a little longer and could take 2-8 weeks

Start by offering both diets at the same time but in separate food bowls, as cats generally don’t like having two different foods mixed in one bowl. Begin by offering 75% of their old diet, with 25% of their new diet

If your cat accepts the new diet well, increase the amount of the new diet being offered and decrease the amount of the old diet over time until only the new diet is being fed

If your cat is more reluctant to accept the new diet, make the change more gradually over a longer period of time by increasing the amount of the new diet only very slowly. Eg, try feeding some of the new food as a treat following a playtime session
