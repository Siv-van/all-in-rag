# Flat-chested kitten syndrome

## Source
https://icatcare.org/articles/flat-chested-kitten-syndrome

---

Flat-chested kittens are born with the deformity, although signs aren’t always present straight away. The sunken or flat chest wall can cause problems with the heart and lungs of affected cats. Signs can worsen as the kitten grows, but once fully grown, the signs tend to settle at a certain level.

## What are the signs of flat-chested kitten syndrome?

#### Affected kittens can show the following signs

- Weight loss or failure to gain weight

- Failure to suckle from the queen (mother cat)

- Increased breathing rate and effort

- Difficulty breathing

- Failure to thrive

- Coughing

- Blue-tinted skin/gums (mucous membranes) due to lack of oxygen (cyanosis)

Most kittens remain bright and active unless severely affected. In kittens that survive, the deformity will become less obvious as the kitten grows and is often not noticeable in adult cats.

The condition varies in severity from very mildly affected kittens to severely affected kittens, where the outlook is very poor.

## What causes flat-chested kitten syndrome?

While the exact cause is unknown, there’s reason to believe that flat-chested kitten syndrome is a genetic condition.

## When to contact your veterinary team

Contact your veterinary team if you have any concerns over your kitten, or if they’re showing any of the signs listed above.

How your vet will diagnose flat-chested kitten syndrome

Your vet will feel some chest deformity when examining your kitten. X-rays and a CT scan can be helpful to understand how severely affected the kitten is.

## What’s the outlook for flat-chested kittens?

The outlook depends on the severity of the condition and whether surgical repair is possible and successful. Your veterinary team will be able to guide you according to your kitten’s situation.

## How is flat-chested kitten syndrome treated?

In kittens showing no signs, treatment may not be required. However, for affected kittens, surgery is often needed to correct the deformity. This is usually carried out at a specialist referral centre.
