# Quality of Life Assessment

## Source
https://icatcare.org/articles/quality-of-life-assessment

---

Quality of life refers to the state of a cat’s life at any given time, as we imagine it perceives it. It’s a balance between positive and negative experiences. A cat with good Quality of Life will have many positive experiences and very few negative ones.

Cats live completely in the present. They can’t understand the idea of suffering now to have a better tomorrow. How a cat feels in the current moment is their quality of life.

## What affects a cat’s Quality of Life?

#### A cat’s quality of life can be influenced by many factors, including

- Physical health and pain - untreated illness or injury directly lowers QoL

- Emotional state or mood – Feeling safe, relaxed and in control supports good quality of life; persistent fear, anxiety, or frustration reduces it.

- Environment - predictability, choice, and opportunities to express natural behaviour

- Presence of other cats - unwanted social pressure or competition

- Presence of people or other animals - can be pleasurable or distressing

## How to assess Quality of Life

Assessing a cat’s quality of life can be challenging. It requires understanding both the species and the individual cat, and then making an assessment based on what you consider that cat’s experience of life may be. Use the quality of life balance scales above to weigh positive and negative experiences and decide where the cat sits on the scale.

If you discover a cat’s quality of life is poor, urgent steps must be taken to improve it. Where euthanasia is the best outcome for a cat, this should be seen as a positive welfare intervention that prevents suffering.

This Quality of Life Guide helps you evaluate each cat’s quality of life and offers simple ways to improve it. But remember, the best thing you can do is move the cat out of confinement and into a permanent environment that meets their needs.

Always focus on the outcome!

Key Points

Quality of Life is the balance between the positive and negative experiences at a point in time

A standardised tool can be used to assess Quality of life that combines an understanding of the species and the individual’s living experience

When Quality of Life is poor, action must be taken quickly to improve it or prevent further suffering
