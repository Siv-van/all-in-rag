# How to tell your cat's age in human years

## Source
https://icatcare.org/articles/how-to-tell-your-cats-age-in-human-years

---

Cats are good at hiding signs of pain or illness, so by seeing their age in human years, we’re able to expect some of the conditions and illnesses they may be more prone to and meet their needs better.

## What are the different life stages for cats?

Cats live on average for around 12-14 years, and it’s not unusual for some to reach their late teens or early twenties. So, what are the six life stages they pass through?

Kitten: 0 - 6 months

During this stage, your cat will be growing quickly and learning about the world around them. Socialisation is important to help them grow into confident adult cats.

Junior: 7 months - 2 years

The junior stage is like teens/young adults in people. By this point, your cat will have reached full size and developed survival instincts.

Adult: 3 – 6 years

By the adult stage, your cat is mature physically and behaviourally. Regular exercise and mental stimulation are important for their physical and mental wellbeing.

Mature: 7 – 10 years

Mature cats are equivalent to humans in their mid-40s to mid-50s and tend to be less active than adult cats.

Senior: 11 – 14 years

By the senior stage, your cat is likely to be less active. They may need help accessing things around the home, for example, a litter tray with lower sides so they can get in and out easily, and ramps to get to favourite sleeping areas if they’re unable to jump.

Super Senior: 15 years+

Many cats reach this stage, and some won’t show any signs of being so senior in age. It’s important to keep a close eye on their behaviour to spot any subtle changes that may indicate something’s not quite right.

## How do I find out the equivalent human age for my cat?

Working out your cat’s age in human years is simple: the first two years of a cat’s life equate to 24 human years and every year thereafter is equivalent to 4 human years.
