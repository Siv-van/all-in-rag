# How to work from home with a cat

## Source
https://icatcare.org/articles/how-to-work-from-home-with-a-cat

---

It’s becoming more common for people to work in remote roles these days. If you have a cat at home, establishing a routine can be highly beneficial in ensuring your days are productive and your cat is happy.

## What are my cat’s possible reactions to me working from home?

How your cat responds to you being at home depends on their personality and their relationship with you – some will look slightly perplexed, if anything at all, and get on with exactly what they do when you’re not there. Others, however, particularly those cats that are really affectionate and demanding when you are normally at home, will see this as a great opportunity to have more of your attention. Setting a routine and ensuring your cat has plenty of space and entertainment can help you both establish a happy and harmonious work from home balance.

## How do I work from home when my cat wants my attention?

#### The most important thing is to try to maintain as many routines as possible – feeding time, playtime and normal mornings and evenings. By doing this, it’s only the bit in the middle of the day, when you don’t leave the house at the usual time, that will feel different for your cat. To make sure you’re able to work freely without disturbing your cat, we recommend the following

Set up your office in a space where your cat doesn’t spend too much time. If your cat wants to spend time with you, make sure they have a comfortable bed in your office, preferably somewhere high up to watch the world go by, such as a cosy blanket placed on a sunny window ledge or a cat tower placed near a window with a view

- Take screen breaks and take the opportunity for a short burst of playtime with your cat. Read our article onplaying with your catfor top tips on how to make the most of your break time together

- Finding yourself distracted? Trypuzzle feedersto keep your cat entertained while you work, and pop some toys and scratching posts around your office – perfect for a good stretch and a scratch after waking from a cat nap

Keep your office space safe – tuck away loose cables and keep mugs of hot drinks away from the edge of your desk (and away from your laptop!). Consider providing your cat with a designated area of your desk, a cosy blanket they can curl up on that can be sprayed with a synthetic feline pheromone to encourage them to use it

- Keep important documents safely stored away, and don’t forget to save your work regularly – an innocent paw on your keyboard could accidentally delete all your hard work in no time

Try to avoid too many extra treats throughout the day – it may be tempting to distract your cat with some tasty titbits if they’re interfering with your work, but over time, this can lead to weight gain and could lead to more attention from your cat as they may expect a treat every time they interact with you. Opt for a fun toy instead, where possible, and never underestimate the value of a simple cardboard box, which can be great for exploring!
