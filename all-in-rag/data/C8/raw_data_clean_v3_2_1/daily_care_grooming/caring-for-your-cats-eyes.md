# Caring for your cat’s eyes

## Source
https://icatcare.org/articles/caring-for-your-cats-eyes

---

Eye problems can quickly become serious in cats, so by regularly checking your cat’s eyes, you’ll soon notice if anything changes.

## What do I need to know about caring for my cat’s eyes?

In a well-lit room, examine your cat’s eyes by checking for discharge, signs of swelling, redness, the presence of the third eyelid, or problems with the eyeball itself (eg, cloudiness). If you notice anything unusual with your cat’s eyes, or if they seem to be in any discomfort (eg, pawing at their eyes, squinting), contact your veterinary team.

## How do I apply eye drops or ointment to my cat’s eyes?

#### Occasionally, your vet may prescribe eye drops or eye ointment for your cat. Follow the steps below to make this as stress-free as possible

Remove discharge from around the eye with a damp cotton wool ball (taking care not to touch the eye itself)

Hold your cat facing away from you, use one hand to tilt their head up

Holding the eyelids open with your free fingers, squeeze the drops or ointment onto the eye, taking care not to let the tip of the dropper/tube touch the surface of your cat’s eye

Gently close your cat’s eye, let them relax, letting go of their head, and as they blink, the drops/ointment will spread across the eye’s surface

It can be easier to apply eye drops or ointment with two people.

## How do I clean my cat’s eyes?

Generally, you shouldn’t need to clean the area around your cat’s eyes. However, if your cat has a very flat face (eg, a Persian), their eyes may water frequently and need wiping regularly.

Gently wipe around your cat’s eyes with a cotton wool ball dampened with clean water, moving from the corner of the eye outwards. Use a separate ball for each eye and dry with a soft tissue.

Take care not to touch the eyeball – this will be painful. and your cat will try to avoid the experience in the future.

## When to contact your veterinary team

If you notice any changes to your cat’s eyes, if they appear to be in discomfort, or if your cat has been prescribed eye drops or ointment and you’re struggling to apply them, contact your veterinary team. Eye problems should be investigated as soon as possible to prevent deterioration and further complications.
