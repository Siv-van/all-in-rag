# Returning

## Source
https://icatcare.org/articles/returning

---

Aim to return cats to the site as quickly as possible after surgery.

Considerations

Males and flank spay females should be returned on the morning following surgery

Extended midline spays, cats with pyometras, and pregnant spays should wait until late the following day or until the second morning

You may need to reassure caregivers that releasing cats so soon after surgery is the right thing to do

Cats should be released into the same place where they were trapped

Avoid severe weather; if you have any concerns, discuss them with your vet, as anaesthesia can impact a cat’s ability to regulate their body temperature

Put food down at the usual site - you can do this yourself or ask the caregivers to meet you so that they can do it. The cats may not eat straight away, but will come back later when people have gone. Sometimes they do not reappear for a day or two if people are present – warn the caregivers that this may be the case, so that they don’t worry

For feral or street cats that find themselves in a homing centre or shelter, return to field (RTF) offers a humane way to shorten confinement and return them to the environment where they were living successfully
