# What can I do to help unowned cats?

## Source
https://icatcare.org/articles/what-can-i-do-to-help-unowned-cats

---

There are millions of unowned cats around the world. Many people want to help cats and there are lots of different options to become involved. ‘Unowned’ cats include cats that have been born and live on the streets, cats that have lived in our homes with us but are no longer wanted or cannot be kept, and pet cats which have lost their way and strayed or been abandoned and need some care. All of these cats need different help based on an understanding of the range of lifestyles which cats lead and their ability to live closely with people (or not).

Neutering is the number one priority

The number one priority to help them is to reduce the population of unowned and unwanted animals and stop it become overwhelming.

A large number of cats on the street can mean many suffer from disease or malnutrition and, because they can cause nuisance and annoy people, they may be cruelly targeted or the local authority may have to step in to reduce numbers. This may not always be done in a humane way. The answer to this is to neuter all pet cats and to have population management plans in place to reduce the number of street and feral cats humanely. Therefore getting the neutering message out there is number 1.

Learn more about cats

Knowledge is so valuable not just in caring for your own cats and keeping them happy and healthy, but can help to understand the challenges of taking the problem of unowned cats. The iCatCare website and other cat charities provide a wealth of helpful information. If you are having problems with your own cat reach out to tackle the issue and enable you to keep your cat. There are also courses which help with understanding of cats.

Give a cat a home

Giving an unowned cat a home can be very satisfying but can become addictive! Your input can help one or two cats, but adding more and more is very likely to be stressful to the cats you already have and may then become overwhelming in their management and costs. Learning more about cats, considering carefully what you can offer for good quality welfare (for both you and the cats) and then looking at how you can help outside your home can allow you to be involved in making a difference for cats.

Join in with the bigger picture

Learn more about unowned cats and how they can be helped. Write to governments, local or national, about cat welfare issues you become aware of. You may feel strongly enough to want to start an organisation yourself, but this requires immense work to do well and legally. It may be there are already good organisations working in the area which are set up and simply need more help and you could join in with them.
