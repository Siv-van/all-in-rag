# Traffic Light Assessment

## Source
https://icatcare.org/articles/traffic-light-assessment

---

Cat Friendly Homing promotes the constant monitoring of cats in care to ensure they remain as well as possible, physically and mentally, while awaiting the move to their future homes. Cats respond very differently to confinement. The Traffic Light Assessment is a simple way to monitor how each cat is coping while in your care.

The Traffic Light Assessment colours

It uses the universal colours red, amber, and green to indicate how comfortable or distressed a cat appears. This helps you identify which cats need your help most urgently and ensures no cat is ever overlooked.

·Red cats:“I need your help right now.”

·Amber cats:“I’m not doing well.”

·Green cats:“I’m doing OK.”

A cat’s colour isn’t fixed - it can change as they settle, and improve or decline during their stay. Regular assessment helps you respond quickly and adapt care to meet the needs of each individual.

A cat’s physical health must be taken into account, as this will impact their ability to cope in a challenging environment, such as a homing centre. Any improvement in physical health may enable a cat to cope better.

Using the Traffic Light Assessment in practice

To decide whether a cat is red, amber, or green, observe their behaviour and body language in the pen or cage, response to people nearby, physical health and any changes over time.

#### Each colour acts as a call to action. For example

A red cat may need fewer interactions, extra hiding places, or a foster home.

#### Cats should be reassessed regularly

Red cats: daily

Amber cats: every 2-3 days

Green cats: every 4-5 days

All red cats will not behave in the same way, nor will amber or green. How each cat shows whether they are coping or not can vary from very active behaviour (e.g., scratching at the door and miaowing loudly) to something much more passive (e.g., sleeping excessively, having a drowsy appearance, or inactivity). It takes practice to identify a cat’s Traffic Light Assessment colour.

Working as a team

The Traffic Light Assessment works best when it’s used consistently across your team. Record and share your observations, discuss each cat’s progress, and agree on practical steps to help them feel more comfortable in your care.

Changes in a cat’s behaviour, posture, or activity may be the first signs that something’s wrong. Just as important, sometimes it’s the absence of behaviour, known asinhibition, that tells you a cat isn’t coping. A cat that appears shut down may be far more distressed than the one that vocalises or paces. Teamwork and good communication are essential to recognise these subtleties.

Remember, the Traffic Light Assessment is a call to action - no cat should remain red. When a cat can’t be made comfortable, despite your best efforts, act quickly to get them out of their current situation and into an environment that better meets their needs.

## Key points

- The Traffic Light Assessment is a simple, Cat Friendly tool to monitor how well each cat is coping in your care.

- Red, amber, and green colours indicate how comfortable or distressed a cat appears and acts as a call to action — no cat should ever remain red.

- Cats should be reassessed regularly: red daily, amber every 2–3 days, green every 4–5 days.

- Each cat expresses stress differently - lack of normal behaviour, or behavioural inhibition, is just as much an indication of poor welfare as overt signs of distress.

- Always consider a cat’s physical health, as this strongly affects how well they cope with confinement.

- Consistent use of the Traffic Light Assessment across the team encourages shared observation, timely action, and ensures no cat is ever overlooked.
