# Follow up

## Source
https://icatcare.org/articles/follow-up

---

Whether a pet home with people or an alternative lifestyle solution is chosen, keeping in touch with the new owners/caregivers is vital. Over time, establishing whether the owner-cat matching process is working effectively is useful, and it also ensures you’re available to the owners/caregivers if any problems arise.

## Recommendations

Giving owners advice before a situation escalates and they consider bringing the cat back to you

Only giving advice that you feel qualified to give. If in doubt, refer owners to their veterinary clinic for advice on health, behaviour, nutrition and general welfare

Referring owners to theadvice section of the iCatCare website, where there is a wealth of general information for cat owners

Making a note of calls or any contact from past adopters so you can follow up to ensure they are getting the support and guidance they need

Increasing your own knowledge through courses, e-learning, reading, and conferences, which will enhance the service that you are already providing to the cat-owning public

Key Points

Follow-up is essential whether cats are adopted as pets, have alternative lifestyles, or are returned to field.

Keep good records of the cats that leave your care and the people and places they go to. Record your follow-up and use the information to make improvements where needed and for the benefit of the cat (s)
