# Which cats should you take in?

## Source
https://icatcare.org/articles/which-cats-should-you-take-in

---

Cat Friendly Homing focuses on helping pet cats— cats that are adapted to living closely with people. These cats benefit from a homing journey because they can live comfortably in a new home where their needs are met.

Taking in every cat quickly leads to overcrowding, which compromises welfare. Admissions should therefore be guided by triage: prioritising urgent cases and offering alternative solutions for cats that would not benefit from coming into care.

## Alternatives to immediate intake

#### Not every cat needs to enter the homing centre. Immediately. Alternatives may include

- Behavioural advice to help a cat remain in its current home

- Neutering and return for suitable strays living comfortably where they are

- TNVR for non-pet cats

- Referral to another organisation with capacity

- Short-term practical or financial support

- Placing the cat on a managed waiting list with regular updates to reassure the owner

## Working with the community

Day-to-day admissions often feel like firefighting. It can help to lift the focus to the bigger picture - tackling the root causes that drive cats into care (e.g. unplanned breeding, barriers to vet access, gaps in owner support, limited homing routes). Building trust takes time and transparency, but even small, consistent steps upstream help reduce the constant pressure on intake.

#### Options include

- Home-to-home adoption to avoid a stay in care

- Practical help so cats can remain with their people (behavioural advice, short-term cost support)

- TNVR to stabilise free-roaming populations

- Supporting caregivers to care for strays in place (neuter, monitor, advise)

- Moving suitable cats straight to foster or in-betweeners directly to alternative-lifestyle homes

- Referring cases to another organisation that has agreed they have capacity

- Partnering with vets, local authorities, community groups and social care on prevention

- Listening to local concerns (e.g., noise, smell, wildlife) to build goodwill, trust and cooperation

- Tracking simple indicators (e.g., reasons for intake, length of stay) to learn what’s working

## Next in the Intake journey: Which cats should you take in?

Key Points

Use triage to prioritise urgent cases

Admit only pet cats and in-betweeners that will benefit from a homing journey

Gather essential information at the start

Keep a clear, managed waiting list

Build a strong foster network

Always consider community-based alternatives, such as TNVR, advice and support to owners, home-to-home adoption, and support for people caring for strays
