# Pen and Cage Setup

## Source
https://icatcare.org/articles/pen-and-cage-setup

---

Cats in homing centres are housed in different ways around the world — from individual pens with designated sleeping and exercise areas in the UK, to stainless-steel cages in the USA, or multi-cat spaces in other regions. Whatever the setup, the goal is the same: meet each cat’s individual needs.

## Single cat housing

Wherever possible, cats should be housed singly or in pairs where familiar cats both enjoy and benefit from the other’s company. Shared accommodation, or housing unfamiliar cats together, adds significant stress in what is already a challenging environment, and even cats with a history of living well together may struggle to maintain a harmonious relationship alongside the pressures of confinement.

#### Every cat should have the following

- Hiding places to give cats a perceived sense of safety, allowing them to retreat from unfamiliar or frightening sights and sounds. Some cats may also benefit from having the front of their pen covered to feel more secure.

- Litter tray, food and water bowls positioned as far away from each other as the space allows to reduce stress.

- Two layers of bedding to help maintain the cat’s familiar scent — when the top blanket becomes soiled, swap it with the clean one underneath and place a fresh one under it.

- Low noise levels to help cats relax and rest.

- Predictable routines for cleaning and feeding so cats can anticipate when things will occur.

- Privacy screens if pens face one another to block direct visual contact with other cats or other animals.

## Multi-cat housing

In certain circumstances, some cats can tolerate living alongside other cats amicably, but they are primarily a solitary species. This means confinement close to unfamiliar cats puts each individual under a lot of additional stress, so group housing should be avoided, if possible.

#### If group housing is unavoidable

Keep groups stable and consistent — ideally no more than six cats, provided space and resources allow.

Where possible, only mix familiar cats with a proven history of cohabitating well together.

To reduce competition, provide ample resources — one of each (litter tray, food bowl, water bowl, bed, hiding place, scratching post, perch) per cat plus one extra.

Distribute resources widely and make use of vertical space with shelves, ramps, and hiding boxes so cats can avoid conflict and choose where to rest.

## Key points

- The Cat Friendly Homing goal is for every cat to be housed singly, or in pairs that cohabit harmoniously.

- Where this isn’t possible, a range of measures must be taken to minimise the distress of multiple cat housing

- Every pen should provide the essentials: places to hide; separate areas for food, water, and toileting; soft bedding that retains familiar scents; and predictable routines.

If group housing is unavoidable, keep groups stable, limit numbers to 6, and provide plenty of well-spaced resources (one per cat plus one), using both floor and vertical space.
