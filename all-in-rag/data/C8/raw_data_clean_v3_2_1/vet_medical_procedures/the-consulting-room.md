# The Consulting Room

## Source
https://icatcare.org/articles/the-consulting-room

---

In a Cat Friendly Clinic, the consulting room requires some careful thought. The following considerations will go a long way to improving the clinic experience for your feline clients.

Ideally, there should be at least one consultation room exclusively or primarily dedicated to feline use – this helps avoid the smell of dogs in the room and so may reduce anxiety for the cat.

If this is not possible, ensure that the room and equipment is thoroughly cleaned between patients.

Consultation Table

Ensure that the table used for consultation has a non-slip surface – this may be achieved by placing a rubber mat or towel/blanket on the table

Insulated bedding on the consultation table

Equipment

Equipment suitable for cats should be available; for example, a cat or paediatric stethoscope, ophthalmoscope, thermometer and accurate weighing scales.

Suitable equipment ready to use

An indirect blood pressure monitor should be available for measuring blood pressure in a conscious cat.

A portable Doppler or oscillometric blood pressure monitor is suitable for this purpose.

In the example to the right, the cat is able to stay in the base of its carrier to have its blood pressure checked.

Consideration for the cat

It is important that consultation times are of sufficient length for cats – allowing the cat time to acclimatise to the room and to settle down is important. We recommend a minimum of 10 minutes (ideally 15 minutes), more for cats undergoing procedures or those known to be anxious in the clinic.

Allow the cat to get out of the carrier of its own accord. If the cat choses to remain in the relative safety of its carrier, carefully remove the top of the carrier and examine the cat in the base. Forcibly removing the cat from the carrier must be avoided.

Examining the cat as it remains in its carrier

Synthetic feline pheromone diffusers are also a useful addition to the consulting room
