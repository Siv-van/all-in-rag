# Polydactyl cats

## Source
https://icatcare.org/articles/polydactyl-cats

---

Polydactyl cats have extra toes on either their front and/or hind feet. The condition has been seen in many breeds of pedigree cats, as well as domestic cats.

Polydactyly explained

Polydactyly is inherited as an autosomal dominant gene defect. The extra toes vary in number and structure, from small pieces of soft tissue to complete functioning digits. In some cases, the additional toes may not be fully functional and may not be connected properly to other bones – this can make them more susceptible to accidental injury.

In some situations, breeders have deliberately tried to perpetuate polydactyl cats (eg, in some lines of the Maine Coon breed, and sometimes by developing a breed defined by the presence of polydactylism – eg, the American Polydactyl), but at International Cat Care, we believe such deliberate breeding of a genetic mutation should be discouraged. While minimally harmful in most cases, there is no reason to deliberately breed cats with this defect for human gratification.

## What are the signs of polydactyly in cats?

Polydactyl cats have extra toes. While this doesn’t always cause any health issues, depending on where the extra toes are positioned, sometimes lameness (limping) or infection can be seen (for example, if a cat’s claw grows around into the pad).

## What causes polydactyly in cats?

Polydactyly is a genetic trait, with the defective gene passed down from the cat’s parents.

## When to contact your veterinary team

If your cat has extra toes and you’re concerned they’re causing your cat a problem, contact your veterinary team for advice.

How your vet will diagnose polydactyly

It’s immediately apparent when a cat is polydactyl, as the extra toes are visible.

## What are the treatment options for polydactyl cats?

Treatment won’t be necessary unless the extra toes are causing your cat any significant health issues or discomfort. If they are, surgery may be recommended to remove the extra toe or toes.
